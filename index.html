About

Nathan Giesbrecht is an independent game creator and programmer.  My goal is to create small, fun experiences anyone can enjoy and to continuously grow creatively. From physical card games to arcade style games to multiplayer experiences, I am excited about all aspects of game design and development.

Over the past 2 years, Nathan has been hard at work studying game creation at NAIT and is dedicated to developing new engaging games to life. With a primary focus on Unity Engine and C# programming, I am always working towards gaining new skills and putting my experience into practice.

Trail Grazer

You have 10 seconds to collect a coin to increase your score. Every time you collect a coin the timer resets and an enemy spawns that follows the exact path you've taken through the stage. Stay alive as long as possible and get a high score! Join the beta on the Google Play store!

Trail Grazer is a fast-paced arcade game in development for mobile devices. It is based off  my very first game jam game created in 2018, and is being continuously updated with quality of life improvements, visual and performance upgrades, and player requested features.

My goal with this project is to learn more about the Android platform, focus on polish and clarity for the player, and to design a highly replayble challenge for players of all skill levels.

Features

  • 2 Levels (More Coming Soon!)
  • Local Save System for Scores and Settings (Online Leaderboards Coming Soon!)
  • Tilt and Virtual Thumbstick Controls
  • Catchy Songs!

Grim Repair

Created with a team for Global Game Jam 2020, Grim Repair is a quick matching game created in under 72 hours.

Return the animals' souls to them before the curtain to the afterlife fully opens!
Use the mouse to drag the matching soul at the bottom of the screen to the animal behind the curtain. Both the animal and pose have to match for the soul to be returned! You get more points the faster you match. The curtain opens faster after each match, and once it's fully open the game is over.

I was the Gameplay Programmer for the team, and was responible for creating the game manager scripts, logic, and controls. It was a great experience working with the team and I learned so much about Unity Events, translating canvas events to the game world, and implementing assets created by other team members with different levels of expereince. It was the first time I had combined mouse interactions in a 3D space, and I am very proud of the results.

Royal Roomble

Royal Roomble is a local multiplayer game prototype for 2-4 players created during NAIT's Game Programming DMIT program. 
Controllers are required.

Place dirt in the arena to guide your knife-wielding Roomba to pop the ballons on your opponents robot vacuums. Be the last Roomba standing, or have the most dirt in your bag when time runs out! 

My primary goal with this prototype was to see how indirect control of an object can be engaging in a competitive environment. I experimented more with having 2D elements on the Unity canvas interact with the 3D Objects, as well as having the game be capable of displaying on multiple monitors if desired.
